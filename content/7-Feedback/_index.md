I wrote this based on what I actually went through in **First Cloud AI Journey** at **AWS Viet Nam**. The goal is not to praise everything, but to show the FCAJ team what worked and what still felt hard for a student like me.

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**  
The learning space was comfortable: questions in the group usually got answers, and materials were enough to start. What was a bit missing was time to know people beyond tech chat. One or two short networking sessions would make it easier to ask for help later.

**2. Support from Mentor / Team Admin**  
Mentors did not hand me the answer right away; they asked me to try first. In the BravelSport review, comments on the high-level diagram, Multi-AZ, and NAT Gateway showed me why the first version still looked like a lab stack. Admin support for schedules and paperwork was fine — I did not wait long.

**3. Fit with my major**  
I study IT at HUTECH, so networking and systems basics still helped. The real new part was cloud: I used to know service names, but this term I finally connected them into **BravelSport** — one sports e-commerce and court-booking site.

**4. Learning opportunities**  
I learned the most from rewriting the architecture after feedback, writing the proposal, and listening to talks at Community Day. Soft skills showed up clearest when I had to explain what I was doing, not just click around in the Console.

**5. Culture & team spirit**  
People shared fairly openly. It felt easier to ask questions than studying alone on YouTube. Community Day also helped me see cloud as more than a personal lab on my laptop.

**6. Policies / benefits**  
The path (worklog → proposal → event → report) was clear, so I usually knew what each week was for. Flexible timing when documentation deadlines stacked up was something I used a lot.

### Additional Questions

- **Most satisfying:** When the BravelSport proposal finally moved in the right direction after mentor feedback — it started to feel like real architecture, not a pile of separate services.
- **What to improve:** Review architecture a bit earlier, and add short sessions on common ECS / networking failures — that was where debugging ate the most time.
- **Would I recommend it?** Yes, if a friend is willing to do labs and write docs. The program is not light, but you learn things you can actually use.

### Suggestions & Expectations

- Give an HA-architecture checklist early in the first proposal round (Multi-AZ, NAT Gateway, monitoring, cleanup) so big rewrites happen less later.
- Add light peer review between people in the same cohort — reading someone else’s diagram teaches a lot.
- I want to keep going toward operations and cost optimization after this bootcamp.
- Thanks to the mentors and FCAJ team for the support during the internship.
